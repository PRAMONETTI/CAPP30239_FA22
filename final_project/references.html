What are the next challenges for Mexican Education Policy?

It has been clear that the pandemic affected many students around the world, and Mexico was not the exception. The pandemic showed us in a higher scale something that, as a society, we already knew; that Mexico is a country with high levels of inequality and where many people do not possess the same resources to overpass periods of crisis.

The pandemic left many students out of classroom for a year and half, and forced at least 435,000 to abandon school. It estimated that as a result of the pandemic, Mexicans will lose on average 1.8 years of scholarity on average4,5. Our goal must be to recover those years and help students to go back to school.

The students who did not leave school, will also face many challenges, since they have lost a lot of valuable time out of classrooms. This represents a lot of learning that needs to be recovered.

It is up to policy leaders to change this trend and guarantee a better education for future generations.
